Historic places

Castle of Good Hope

The castle is known as the oldest surviving structure in South Africa is about 342 years. It was home to freeburghers and slaves ,also served as a lodge for visitors traveling to the east Indies. It also acted as local headquaters for the South African army.

District 6 museum

The museum has an impressive collection of historic materials including photographs, paintings, street signs and audio visual recordings of district 6. It was established to commemorate the district 6 community that was scattered when the aphartheid government declared it a whites only.

Cape Town City Hall

This is the first place from where Nelson Mandela spoke after being release from prison in 1990. The city hall's carillon was installed as a world war I war memorial, Today it has become a central venue for creative and cultural events.
